# 1. Project Overview

## What are you trying to do?
Build **Iatris**, a web-based dispatch platform that connects people in underserved communities to nearby **Community Health Workers (CHWs)** for basic healthcare services. 

Think **Uber meets healthcare** for areas where clinics are too far, too expensive, or too overwhelmed. Supported services include:
- Symptom triage
- Medication delivery
- Wound care
- Prenatal checkups
- Health education

## Who is this for?
There are two primary user types, alongside secondary administrative users.

### 1. Patients / Requesters
People in rural or underserved areas who need basic health services but can't easily access a clinic. Examples include:
- A pregnant woman needing a prenatal check.
- An elderly person needing wound care.
- A parent with a sick child.

### 2. Community Health Workers (CHWs)
Trained volunteers or paid workers already embedded in communities (WHO estimates 5+ million globally). They need a system to:
- Receive requests.
- Manage their schedule.
- Log visits.

### 3. Secondary Users (NGO Administrators)
Administrators at GNEC subsidiaries who:
- Oversee CHW networks.
- Track health trends in the community.
- Allocate resources appropriately based on data.

## What problems does this solve?
- **Lack of Access:** 400+ million people globally lack access to essential health services.
- **Scattered Dispatching:** CHWs exist but are dispatched inefficiently—often paper-based, phone-call-based, or walk-in only.
- **Resource Misallocation:** There is no centralized data on community health needs, preventing NGOs from allocating resources effectively.
- **Awareness Gaps:** Patients don't know which CHWs are available, nearby, or qualified for their specific health issue.

## What does the product do?
1. **Request:** A patient submits a health request detailing their symptoms, urgency, and location.
2. **Triage & Match:** The system triages the urgency, finds the nearest available CHW with the appropriate skills, and dispatches them.
3. **Tracking:** The system tracks the visit from request to completion.
4. **Data Logging:** After the visit, health outcome data is logged for the NGO to use for future resource planning.
